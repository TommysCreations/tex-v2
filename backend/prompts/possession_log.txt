You are charting one chunk of basketball game film for scouting. Scouted team: {scouted_team}. Opponent: {opponent}. Seeded {scouted_team} roster (jersey -> name) is provided separately; use jersey numbers. This is production (tripod/Hudl) footage and likely has NO on-screen game clock — do not invent one.

Task: produce a chronological log of every OFFENSIVE possession by the scouted team ({scouted_team}) that is visible in THIS chunk. One entry per {scouted_team} offensive possession.

For each possession capture:
- video_elapsed: mm:ss from the START of THIS chunk's video, at the moment the possession begins.
- personnel: the {scouted_team} jerseys on the floor that you can confidently identify (up to 5). If you can only ID some, list those and stop. NEVER invent a jersey to reach five.
- action_type: closest match from [set_play, PnR, DHO, iso, transition, motion, five_out, box_set, horns, flex, press_break, other]. Use "other" + detail if none fits.
- action_detail: short free-text description of what actually happened.
- situation: one of [BLOB, SLOB, ATO, late_clock, end_of_period, press_break, none].
- initiator: jersey of the {scouted_team} player who CREATED the possession (see INITIATOR DEFINITION below), or "unknown".
- screeners: jerseys who set screens this possession, or [].
- outcome_code: one of ["2pt make","2pt miss","3pt make","3pt miss","FT (N-of-M)","TO (cause)","OREB -> <next outcome>"].
- outcome_detail: free text (shooter, location, cause, etc.).
- boundary: "complete", or "start_partial" if already in progress when the chunk opens, or "end_partial" if cut off when the chunk ends.
- confidence: high/medium/low for your jersey + attribution certainty on this possession.
- notes: anything else worth recording.

INITIATOR DEFINITION — who counts as the initiator:
- The initiator is the player who CREATES the play: the one who triggers the scoring action (starts the drive, makes the move that breaks down the defense, attacks off the catch).
- It is NOT the player who simply brings the ball up the court. Ball-advancement alone never makes a player the initiator.
- When the ball-bringer hands off or passes to a different player who then creates the action, the initiator is that second player.

DISCIPLINE — read twice. COMPLETENESS and HONESTY both apply at the same time; neither one overrides the other.

COMPLETENESS — account for the whole chunk:
- Log the scouted team's offensive action across the ENTIRE chunk, in order, from the first sequence to the last. Do NOT silently skip possessions.
- Messy, fast, scrambled, broken, and no-shot sequences all count as observed offensive action and MUST appear in the log. A turnover, a blocked shot, a loose ball that goes out of bounds, and a dead-ball reset each count as a real sequence and get a row.
- Exact possession boundaries are not critical. If a scrambled sequence is logged as one entry or split into two, either is acceptable, as long as the action is observed and recorded.
- What matters is that NO real offensive sequence goes entirely unlogged — NOT that your possession count matches any specific number. Omitting a sequence that clearly occurred is an error, exactly as serious as inventing one.

HONESTY — only what you can see:
- Log ONLY what you can see. Do NOT synthesize. No tendencies, no counts, no player profiles, no pace assessment. Just the possessions, in order.
- If you cannot cleanly determine the initiator, a jersey, the action, or the outcome for a sequence that clearly occurred, STILL log it as a row: write "unknown" in the fields you cannot read and set confidence=low. A logged possession with unknown fields is correct and desired.
- Do NOT invent details to fill a row, and do NOT drop a possession just because you cannot read its details. A confident guess in a field is a failure; so is omitting a real possession.

- Do not stitch across the chunk edge. Flag partials and move on.

Return JSON ONLY — an array of possession objects in the shape above. No prose, no markdown fences.
